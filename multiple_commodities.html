Information on Various Commodities

Below are brief descriptions of the commodities we cover:

  • Gram Dal: A staple pulse, high in protein and used in various dishes.
  • Sugar: A sweetener derived from sugarcane or sugar beet, used in cooking and baking.
  • Rice: A primary staple food for over half of the world's population, consumed in various forms.
  • Wheat: A cereal grain used to make flour for bread, pasta, and many other foods.
  • Atta: Whole wheat flour, commonly used in Indian cooking for making flatbreads.
  • Tur/Arhar Dal: Yellow split pigeon peas, commonly used in soups and curries.
  • Urad Dal: Black gram lentils, rich in protein and often used in South Indian dishes.
  • Moong Dal: Green gram lentils, known for their high protein content and easy digestibility.
  • Masoor Dal: Red lentils, quick to cook and often used in soups and stews.
  • Gur: Jaggery, a traditional unrefined sugar made from sugarcane or palm sap.
  • Groundnut Oil: Oil extracted from peanuts, used for frying and cooking.
  • Mustard Oil: An oil made from mustard seeds, popular in Indian cuisine.
  • Vanaspati: A hydrogenated fat used in cooking and baking.
  • Sunflower Oil: A healthy oil extracted from sunflower seeds, commonly used in frying and salads.
  • Soya Oil: Extracted from soybeans, widely used in food production.
  • Palm Oil: A versatile oil used in cooking, food production, and non-food items.
  • Tea: A popular beverage derived from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant.
  • Milk: A nutritious liquid produced by mammals, consumed as a beverage and used in cooking.
  • Salt: A mineral essential for human health and food preservation.
  • Potato: A starchy tuber, versatile in cooking and a staple food in many diets.
  • Onion: A common vegetable used for flavoring in a variety of dishes.
  • Tomato: A juicy fruit commonly used in salads, sauces, and cooking.
